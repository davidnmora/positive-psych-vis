One fine day in the late 90s, a psychologist snaps at his young daughter.

She snaps back.

From this moment sprouts an entirely new field of psychology. A field unlike anything before it.

As you read this, hundreds of psychologists around the world are hard at work in this new field: conspiring to unlock the secrets of what makes us happy, healthy, creative, and resilient.

What they're discovering will quietly shape the future of humanity.

This is a story of how they came to be.

This is the story of

Positive

Psychology

Back to the grumpy psychologist and his daughter.

His name is Martin Seligman. And when he snaps at his daughter, she hits him with a retort that sends all of psychology realing:

"Daddy, stop being so grumpy. I used to be a whiney baby, but I learned to handle myself. So you learn to be less grumpy, too."

Mindflowing, right? Yeah, not so much...

To understand why this was so world-shaking for Martin Seligman, realize that up to now he's spent his life studying people who feel depressed, fearful, and helpless.

Cheery stuff, right?

I mean, imagine the the rush of purpose and joy Seligman must have felt when he published his big paper, "Chronic fear produced by unpredictable electric shock."

But this focus on human disfunction isn't surprising: up until now,psychology essentially comprised of trying to diagnose and fix super messed up people.

So it's no surprise Martin Seligman ran around electricuting people into chronic fear.

But it got more depressing. Seligman started to realize that people weren't just inflicted with bad psychology: actually, otherwise healthy people were actively learning behaviors which steam rolled their own mental health.

Enter Seligman's no-nonsense daughter.

Her retort sent him in a new direction: if he'd uncovered ways people learned to wreck their mind, could he discover ways that people could learn to build happier, healthier minds?

Oh, yeah, and then he got elected president of the American Psychological Association.

Things were about to change...
